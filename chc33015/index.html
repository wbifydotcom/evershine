CHC33015 Certificate III in Individual Support

Course Overview

We are offering Australia's leading Aged Care Course “Certificate III in Individual Support”. This course is designed in a way that can fit in every student’s lifestyle and you can complete your qualification online from anywhere in Australia. If you are passionate about working in health industry and planning for amazing job opportunities in nursing profession. Then, this course will help you to achieve your desired goals.

This qualification reflects the role of community and residential home care settings, who follow an individualised plan to provide person-centred support to people who may require assistance due to ageing, disability, or other factors. We offer Certificate III in Individual Support under three sub streams; Ageing, Disability and Home and Community.

Entry requirements

Although there are no pre-requisites for this qualification, learners at Evershine College must meet the following entry requirements to obtain admission in this training product:

Are 18 years or older
Have sound language and literacy skills (at least Year 10 English, or equivalent)
Have basic computer skills

Students are required to

Obtain a Unique Student Identifier (USI)
Provide a valid ID with his/her picture and signature
Undertake a Language, Literacy and Numeracy Evaluation; and
Undertake a Personal Learning Plan Evaluation before accessing the course materials and commencing studies
Obtain a positive National Criminal History check

Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)

The RPL pathway is available to any learner who can provide evidence of competency for the units offered within the qualification.

Language Literacy and numeracy

To assist in identifying any special learning needs, you are required to provide Evershine College with information regarding your LLN requirements on your enrolment form, prior to the start of the training program. If you do have any learning difficulties, you are encouraged to discuss these with your course instructor or facilitator either prior to course commencement or during the course induction. Be assured that discussions with our staff will be treated as Confidential.

Student Placements

All students are required to complete student placement for 120 hours in an aged care facility as a mandatory requirement of this course.

NOTE: Student are required to provide evidence of police check, vaccination proof, manual handling training prior to commencement of their student placement. Students has to pay separately for the costs involved to complete these requirements.

How will I be assessed?

The CHC33015 – Certificate III in Individual Support (Ageing) is a nationally accredited course of study. During the course you will be given clear and specific instructions on what you must learn, how you'll be assessed and what you must do to show you are ">competent". To earn your qualification, you must be assessed as competent in all the nationally accredited units required by your qualification.

Study Hours and Assessment

Expected Study Hours: There are up to approximately 1010 study hours for each core unit of this course (including Vocational Placement), depending on your industry experience and relevant knowledge. You may work at your own pace as an online training student and complete your course faster if you'd like!

Assessment Methods: Assessment methods for this course include a combination of questioning, product-based methods, portfolio, direct observation, and third-party evidence. These will give you maximum opportunity to develop your knowledge in individual support and specialise in ageing or aged care.

Assessment Arrangement

Students are required to complete classroom assessments and workplace assessments. Classroom assessment tasks include:

Written questions
Role play
Case study
Research Report
Project

Workplace assessment tasks include;

Workplace observation
Workplace project
Supervisor report
Portfolio

Enrolment Period

The CHC33015 course runs all throughout the year. This allows you to enrol and start your course straight away or on your preferred commencement date.

Course Duration: You will have up to 12 months starting from your commencement date to complete the course. Students can enrol anytime in the year under this delivery method.
